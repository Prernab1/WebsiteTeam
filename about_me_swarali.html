My Role Model

My grandmother, with whom I share my birthday! I lovingly call her Akki. She grew up with 7 siblings in a modest but disciplined household. Because she was born under the British rule in India, she learnt in an English school and can speak the language to this day (she's turning 70 this year!). She studied till 10th grade (a big deal at the time), and because she saw how hard her parents worked for her and her siblings, she always strove to earn money of her own and contribute to her family. First she sowed clothes and sold them for meagre sums of money, then she learnt typewriting and began an illustrious career writing legal documents in the government. Even after she left her job after marriage (as was common in Indian society), she saved diligently to ensure a flow of money when times were tough. Under her motherly shadow, my mother won several awards in drama, dance and even released a music cassette (modern day equivalent would be Youtube Vevo), not to mention top her boards and college degree. Of course, my mother is also my role model, but she is who she is only because of my Akki.
